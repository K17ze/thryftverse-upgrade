[5|First Paragraph] ThryftVerse is a mobile-first, resale-focused social marketplace built on React Native (Expo) with a Fastify/PostgreSQL backend. This report is the outcome of a full-codebase audit of the frontend — 115 screens, ~40 shared component families, 27 API service modules, a 5-tab navigation architecture, and a 110-route stack — cross-referenced against the interaction patterns, information architecture, and business logic of the strongest players in two categories:
[6|Compact] Flagship social/visual apps: Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok — the benchmark for feed engagement, content creation, visual discovery, and retention mechanics.
[7|Compact] Marketplace leaders: eBay, Vinted, Depop, Poshmark, Grailed, StockX, Whatnot — the benchmark for listing flows, trust & safety, buyer/seller protection, pricing intelligence, and monetisation.
[8|Heading 2] 1.1 Where ThryftVerse is already strong
[9|First Paragraph] The audit found a product that is substantially more ambitious than a typical MVP marketplace. Several capabilities already exceed what Vinted or Depop shipped at comparable maturity:
[10|Compact] Fractional co-ownership (“Co-Own”) of assets — an entire trading vertical (asset detail, order book, portfolio, leaderboard, buyout, syndicates) that no mainstream fashion marketplace offers. This is a genuine differentiator closer to StockX-meets-Public.com.
[11|Compact] Live auctions with countdowns, sticky bid docks, seller auction centre, and reserve pricing — comparable in scope to Whatnot’s core loop (minus live video).
[12|Compact] A rich messaging platform — group chats, bots and a bot builder, message requests, quick replies, voice messages, reactions, reply-quotes, link previews, shared media galleries, archived/muted conversation management. This is beyond what any competitor marketplace ships (Vinted/Depop chat is rudimentary).

[14|Compact] Design-system discipline — design tokens, premium form primitives, flagship component library, skeleton loaders everywhere, haptics, Reanimated/Skia-based animation, i18n string extraction tooling, and CI-style guardrail tests for scroll/animation usage.
[15|Compact] Poster stories (ephemeral story-like content), pulse feed, outfit builder, style quiz, visual search entry point, and personalisation surfaces.

[41|Heading 1] 3. Navigation & Information Architecture Audit
[42|Heading 2] 3.1 Current architecture
[43|First Paragraph] The 5-tab layout (Home / Explore / Create-FAB / Inbox / Profile) mirrors Instagram’s canonical structure and is the right skeleton. Strengths: haptic tab feedback, unread badge on Inbox, custom raised Create button, hide-on-keyboard, accessibility labels on every tab.
[44|Heading 2] 3.2 Gaps vs flagship IA
[45|Compact] Explore tab conflates search and browse. Instagram and Pinterest separate the feed of discovery (Explore grid / Pinterest home) from search intent (a search screen you commit to). ThryftVerse’s Explore tab is a search screen with three content tabs (Pulse/Looks/Edit); the masonry discovery grid lives in components but there is no dedicated infinite visual-browse surface as the tab’s default state. Resolution: make Explore default to an infinite, personalised masonry grid (Pinterest-style, PinterestMasonryGrid already exists); tapping the search bar transitions to search-intent mode with recent searches, trending queries, and category chips.
[46|Compact] No dedicated “Selling” or “Activity” surface at top level. Vinted/Depop sellers live in their selling dashboard. ThryftVerse buries MyListings/SellerFulfilment/SellerAuctionCentre under Profile. Resolution: a “Seller Hub” entry (Profile top-cell or a swappable 5th tab for power sellers) aggregating listings, orders-to-ship, offers, auctions, analytics, and payouts.
[47|Compact] Notifications have no tab or persistent affordance. NotificationsList exists as a pushed screen. Instagram places activity behind a heart icon in the top bar with a badged counter. Resolution: persistent bell/heart icon with unread badge in Home and Profile headers; group notifications by type (offers, likes, follows, orders, price drops).
[48|Compact] Deep-link coverage is minimal (2 references). Flagships treat every entity (listing, profile, look, auction, collection) as a shareable URL. Resolution: define a universal-link scheme (thryftverse.com/l/:id, /u/:handle, /look/:id, /auction/:id), wire Linking config into the navigator, and generate OG-card web previews server-side.
[49|Compact] Back-stack hygiene for cross-vertical flows. TradeHub, AuctionHome, and CoOwnHub are parallel universes reachable from multiple entry points; there is no persistent “mode switcher”. Resolution: a top-level segment (Shop / Auctions / Co-Own) on Home, preserving one mental model.
[50|Compact] No global cart. Buy-now is item-at-a-time. Depop and eBay both support multi-item bags per seller (bundles). Resolution: a lightweight per-seller bag enabling bundle checkout and combined shipping (see 5.4).

[53|Heading 2] 4.1 Onboarding & Authentication
[54|First Paragraph] Current: AuthLanding → Login/SignUp, ForgotPassword, ChangePassword, TwoFactorSetup, ActiveSessions. Style quiz exists (StyleQuiz) but is a route, not an onboarding step.
[55|Body Text] Gaps: - No social sign-in (Apple/Google/Facebook). Vinted, Depop, and Instagram all lead with OAuth; email-first sign-up measurably increases drop-off. Apple Sign-In is an App Store requirement if any third-party login is added. - No progressive onboarding: flagships collect interests (Pinterest’s topic picker is legendary), sizes (Vinted asks sizes to power its feed), and brands during first-run, then immediately demonstrate a personalised feed. - No “guest browse” nudge strategy — the point at which an anonymous user is asked to register (on like, on message, on checkout) should be deliberate and delightful, not a hard wall. - No handle/username availability check with suggestions during sign-up. - No welcome/empty-feed education (first-session tooltips, coach marks for Create button, co-own disclosure primer).
[56|Body Text] Resolutions: 1. Add Apple + Google OAuth (backend auth provider columns exist conceptually; add /auth/oauth/:provider endpoints). 2. Make StyleQuiz + size/brand picker a skippable 3-step first-run flow feeding Personalisation; show the feed reshaping live as the user picks (Pinterest’s trick). 3. Deferred registration: allow browse + like locally, sync on account creation. 4. Real-time username validation with suggestions.
[57|Heading 2] 4.2 Home / Discovery Feed
[58|First Paragraph] Current: HomeScreen (1,688 LOC) with hero carousel (currently placeholder editorial slots: “The Archive Drop”, “Summer Layers”, “Streetwear Daily”), editorial sections, visual category tiles, featured boards, Pinterest-style masonry grid, poster-story rail, skeleton loading, staggered grid entrance animations.
[59|Body Text] Gaps vs Instagram/Pinterest: - Curation is static, not learned. The hero slots are hardcoded editorial placeholders navigating to generic Browse. Pinterest’s home is 100% behaviourally ranked; Instagram’s feed mixes followed content with ranked recommendations. ThryftVerse has the personalisation plumbing (style graph, personalisation screen, saved-affinity ExploreCollection source) but the home feed doesn’t visibly re-rank on behaviour. - No “following” feed. A social marketplace’s killer retention surface is new items from sellers you follow (Depop’s feed is exactly this). The social graph (follow/block) now works in the backend — the feed consuming it doesn’t exist. - No engagement affordances on feed cards beyond like: no long-press quick-actions (share, see similar, hide, add to collection — Pinterest’s long-press wheel), no “seller is live/new” indicators, no price-drop badges on cards. - No pull-to-refresh discovery narrative — flagships use refresh as a “new drop” moment (fresh hero, “N new items from people you follow”). - Poster stories rail exists but lacks the Instagram mechanics that make stories work: unwatched-first ordering with gradient rings (AvatarRing exists in chat components — reuse it), auto-advance between users’ stories, reply-to-story → DM, story insights for creators. - No infinite feed end-state — flagships never dead-end; when a section exhausts, inject “more like the items you liked”.
[60|Body Text] Resolutions: 1. Ship a two-tab home: “For You” (ranked) / “Following” (chronological from followed sellers + friends’ likes, powered by the now-working social graph). 2. Convert hero carousel to a server-driven campaign system (/feed/campaigns) so merchandising is operable without releases. 3. Add long-press quick-action sheet on every ProductCardV2: See similar · Add to collection · Share · Not interested — and feed “not interested” back into ranking. 4. Card-level signals: price-drop badge, “3 others watching”, “new from @seller you follow”. 5. Story-ring elevation: unwatched-first, reply-to-story, view counts for the poster.
[61|Heading 2] 4.3 Search & Explore
[62|First Paragraph] Current: SearchScreen with Pulse/Looks/Edit tabs, GlobalSearchScreen, VisualSearchScreen (entry exists), Filter screen, CategoryTree, Browse.
[63|Body Text] Gaps: - No saved searches with alerts — the single highest-retention feature in Vinted/eBay. Zero references in the codebase. - No recent-searches / trending-searches state on search focus (table stakes in every flagship). - No query understanding chips — Vinted decomposes “nike air max 90 size 9” into brand/model/size filter chips; ThryftVerse passes raw strings. - Visual search is an entry point without a highlighted loop — Pinterest Lens is a first-class camera button in the search bar; results should blend exact matches with style-similar items and shoppable looks. - Filters lack persistence and profiles — “my size” filter memory (Vinted’s killer default: only show my sizes) doesn’t exist. - No search results merchandising — zero-result states should recover with near-matches, broader category, and a one-tap “create alert for this search”.
[64|Body Text] Resolutions: 1. Saved searches + alerts end-to-end: save current query+filters, backend cron matches new listings, push notification “3 new matches for ‘carhartt jacket M’”. This is the #1 retention build. 2. Search-focus state: recent (locally stored + server-synced), trending (from search logs), category shortcuts. 3. Size-profile default filter (“Only my sizes” toggle persisted in Personalisation). 4. Promote Lens-style camera icon inside the AppSearchBar; blend visual-similar results. 5. Zero-result recovery module with “Save this search” CTA.
[65|Heading 2] 4.4 Product Detail Page (PDP)
[66|First Paragraph] Current: ItemDetail composed of a strong component set: ProductMediaGallery, FullscreenMediaViewer, ProductIdentitySummary, ProductAttributeChips, ProductCommerceSummary, SellerTrustCard, ProductPolicySheet, RecommendationRail, SeenInLooksRail, DoubleTapHeart, share sheet.
[67|Body Text] Gaps vs eBay/Vinted/Depop/StockX: - No price history / market context. StockX’s price chart and “last sold” data drive confidence; ThryftVerse has a market ledger for co-own assets but nothing on standard listings. Even Vinted shows “price dropped from £X”. - No size guide (0 references) — a conversion killer for apparel; Depop/ASOS-style size guide sheet per category + brand. - No bundle prompt — “Add more from this seller and save on shipping” (Vinted’s bundle entry lives on the PDP). - No visual-similar grid — Pinterest and Depop both show “more like this” visually below the item; the RecommendationRail exists but a tap-to-expand similarity grid from the item’s photos is the flagship pattern. - Offer affordance placement — Make-Offer exists as a screen; flagship pattern is offer + buy-now as twin sticky CTAs with the offer sheet showing seller’s floor hints (“offers above £X are usually accepted”). - No watcher/scarcity signals — eBay’s “12 watchers”, Vinted’s “5 people are interested”. Drives urgency honestly. - No authentication/verification badge programme for high-value items (StockX/Grailed authenticity is the entire brand promise). Co-own has auth photos; standard listings don’t surface any verification tier. - No buyer-protection strip — Vinted’s PDP shows the Buyer Protection line item right under price; this is trust-critical (see 5.2). - Reviews shown are seller-level only; no item Q&A — eBay’s ask-a-question drives conversion on ambiguous items; ThryftVerse has chat but no public Q&A on listing.
[68|Body Text] Resolutions (PDP build list): price-drop history line, category size-guide sheet, bundle upsell row, “More like this” visual grid, watcher counts, verification badge tier, buyer-protection strip, sticky twin CTAs (Offer / Buy now) with haptic press states, public Q&A section (optional P2).
[69|Heading 2] 4.5 Sell / Listing Flow
[70|First Paragraph] Current: SellScreen (1,420 LOC) — photos via SortablePhotoStrip, media studio, title/description/price/original-price, brand, size, condition (4 tiers), category, up to 8 tags, listing mode selector (sell-now / auction / co-own), shipping method/payer, auction params, co-own share params, draft persistence, preview screen, success screen, publication pipeline with recoverable failure states.
[71|Body Text] This is already one of the strongest sell flows in the category — the mode selector unifying standard/auction/fractional is unique.
[72|Body Text] Gaps: - No AI listing assist. Vinted and eBay now auto-suggest title, category, brand, colour, and price from the first photo. ThryftVerse has an ml-service in its stack — wiring photo → attribute suggestions is the single biggest seller-side win. - No pricing intelligence — “similar items sold for £18–24” (eBay/Vinted show sold-comps at pricing step). The market-ledger infrastructure could power this. - No video on listings (G1) — Depop supports listing video; it measurably lifts sell-through. - No bulk/multi-listing tools, no relist/duplicate action for power sellers. - No boost/bump at publish — the natural monetisation moment (“Bump for £0.95 to reach 5× more buyers” — Vinted’s model) is absent. - No package-size selection feeding real shipping rates and label generation (see 5.5). - Condition scale is coarse — 4 options; Vinted uses 5 with photo-anchored descriptions; add per-condition helper text and photo examples. - No listing quality meter — a “completeness score” (photos ≥4, measurements, tags) that predicts sell-through and gamifies quality.
[73|Body Text] Resolutions: photo-first AI autofill (P0), sold-comps price hint (P0), listing video (P1), publish-moment boost upsell (P1), duplicate/relist (P1), package-size + label pipeline (P0, see 5.5), quality meter (P2).
[74|Heading 2] 4.6 Offers, Negotiation & Bundles
[75|First Paragraph] Current: MakeOffer screen; OfferBubble in chat; offers ride the messaging rails.
[76|Body Text] Gaps: - No structured counter-offer loop with expiries visible to both parties (eBay best-offer: offer → counter → counter, 48h expiry timers). - No offer-to-likers — Poshmark’s most famous mechanic: sellers send a private discount to everyone who liked an item, paired with a shipping discount. Requires likes list per listing (exists) + a seller-side “send offer” action. - No bundle offers — Vinted’s bundle flow (buyer multi-selects from a seller’s wardrobe, seller sets bundle discount tiers like 10% off 2 items) is a proven AOV driver. - No auto-accept / auto-decline thresholds for sellers (eBay lets sellers set floors so offers resolve instantly).
[77|Body Text] Resolutions: structured offer state machine (pending/countered/expired/accepted) with timers rendered in chat OfferBubble; seller offer-to-likers composer; per-seller bundle bag + discount tiers; seller floor settings in ManageListing.
[78|Heading 2] 4.7 Checkout, Orders & Post-Purchase
[79|First Paragraph] Current: Checkout (1,388 LOC) with address + payment selectors and add-card/add-address sheets, Success, order history, OrderDetail, OrderReceipt, OrderSupport, SellerFulfilment, WriteReview. Capability layer references stripe_americas region gating.
[80|Body Text] Gaps: - No Apple Pay / Google Pay — one-tap wallets are the single largest mobile checkout conversion lever; all competitors support them. - No buyer-protection/escrow narrative at checkout — the fee line and “your money is held until you confirm receipt” copy is what makes strangers transact (see 5.2). - No promo/discount code field (15 scattered “promo” references, no redemption flow). - Order tracking is data without a map/timeline visual — flagship post-purchase shows a stepper (Ordered → Shipped → In transit → Delivered) with carrier events; 20 tracking references exist, so data plumbing is partial — the visual timeline and push updates on state change are the gap. - No returns/refund request flow — OrderSupport exists but a structured “Request refund → reason → evidence photos → seller response window → escalate” flow (Vinted’s model) is missing. - No delivery-confirmation nudge — Vinted asks the buyer “Everything OK?” 2 days after delivery, auto-releasing escrow after N days; drives review volume too.
[81|Body Text] Resolutions: wallet payments (P0), buyer-protection line + escrow copy (P0), visual tracking timeline with push (P1), structured refund/dispute flow (P0 — see 5.6), promo code redemption (P2), post-delivery confirmation + auto-release (P0).

[110|Heading 1] 5. Marketplace Business-Logic Gap Analysis (vs eBay, Vinted, Depop, Poshmark, StockX)
[111|First Paragraph] This section examines the rules of the marketplace rather than screens.
[112|Heading 2] 5.1 Fee & monetisation model
[113|First Paragraph] Competitors: Vinted — free to sell, buyer pays “Buyer Protection” fee (5% + £0.70) + optional item verification fee; monetises bumps/wardrobe spotlight. Depop — moved to buyer-fee model + boosted listings (8% on boosted sales). eBay — final-value fees + Promoted Listings (2–15% ad rate). Poshmark — flat 20% commission, monetises via engagement mechanics (parties, offers).
[114|Body Text] ThryftVerse today: trade fees exist in co-own; standard-listing monetisation surfaces (bumps, boosts, spotlight) are absent.
[115|Body Text] Gap & resolution: define and implement at least two revenue rails: (1) buyer-protection fee at checkout (trust + revenue in one move, see 5.2), and (2) seller visibility products — bump (one-off re-rank), closet spotlight (7-day category placement). Both need: purchase sheet, receipt, effect visualisation (“your listing reached 4.2× more buyers” — the retention loop that makes sellers rebuy).
[116|Heading 2] 5.2 Buyer protection & escrow
[117|First Paragraph] Competitors: Vinted holds payment until buyer confirms (2-day auto-release post-delivery); refund guaranteed if item doesn’t arrive / isn’t as described. eBay MBG is the industry’s strongest promise. This is the precondition for stranger-to-stranger commerce.
[118|Body Text] ThryftVerse gap: no user-visible protection programme: no protection copy at PDP/checkout, no escrow-hold explanation, no “confirm receipt” step, no not-as-described claim flow.
[119|Body Text] Resolution (P0): (a) escrow-hold order states (paid → shipped → delivered → buyer-confirmed/auto-released → paid-out); (b) PDP + checkout protection strip; (c) confirm-receipt prompt with review capture; (d) claims flow with evidence upload and seller response window; (e) clear seller-facing payout timeline.
[120|Heading 2] 5.3 Trust, verification & moderation
[121|First Paragraph] Competitors: Vinted ID verification + phone verification badges; StockX/Grailed authentication as a paid service; eBay Authenticity Guarantee for sneakers/watches/handbags above thresholds.
[122|Body Text] ThryftVerse: report/block flows exist and now function (fixed last session); AML alerting exists on the backend (unusually sophisticated); but no user-visible verification tiers, no authentication service for high-value items, no counterfeit-detection assist at listing time.
[123|Body Text] Resolution: verified-profile badge (email+phone+ID tiers) (P1); “ThryftVerse Verified” authentication opt-in for items above a price threshold, leveraging the co-own auth-photo pipeline that already exists (P2); listing-time brand/counterfeit heuristics via ml-service (P2).
[124|Heading 2] 5.4 Bundles & multi-item economics
[125|First Paragraph] Vinted bundles are a core AOV lever: buyer selects several items from one wardrobe → seller-defined bundle discounts → single combined-shipping checkout. ThryftVerse has 9 bundle references but no buyer-facing flow. Resolution: per-seller bag, seller discount tiers (e.g. 2 items −10%, 3+ −15%), combined shipping calculation, one payment intent, one shipment. This also fixes the “no cart” IA gap (3.2-6).
[126|Heading 2] 5.5 Shipping & label logistics
[127|First Paragraph] Competitors: Vinted’s integrated label generation (buyer picks carrier/drop-off point at checkout, seller gets a QR/label) removed the single largest friction in C2C selling. eBay/Poshmark ship labels by default.
[128|Body Text] ThryftVerse: shipping method/payer fields exist on listings; 1 reference to shipping labels; no rate shopping, no label generation, no drop-off point picker, no tracking ingestion loop.
[129|Body Text] Resolution (P0 for the selling loop): integrate a shipping aggregator (e.g. Shippo/EasyPost-class API): package size on listing → live rates at checkout → prepaid label to seller → tracking webhook → order timeline events → auto “delivered” → escrow clock. Until then, ship a manual-tracking-number entry UX with carrier detection (cheap interim).
[130|Heading 2] 5.6 Disputes, returns & resolution centre
[131|First Paragraph] eBay’s Resolution Centre pattern: structured case types (not received / not as described), evidence, deadlines, escalation to platform, outcomes logged to seller standards. ThryftVerse: OrderSupport + support tickets exist — but generic tickets, not structured cases with timers and money movement. Resolution: case objects bound to orders with state machines, buyer/seller evidence steps, countdown UI, and automated refund execution on resolution (P0-lite: not-received + not-as-described only).
[132|Heading 2] 5.7 Seller standards & performance
[133|First Paragraph] eBay seller levels (Top Rated), Vinted’s reliability signals, Poshmark ambassador status — performance programmes that convert good behaviour into visibility. ThryftVerse: ratings exist; no standards engine. Resolution: compute shipping-time, cancellation-rate, rating aggregates → badge tiers → feed-ranking boost + PDP badge (P1).
[134|Heading 2] 5.8 Tax & regulatory compliance logic
[135|First Paragraph] Marketplaces in UK/EU must report seller income (DAC7/UK equivalents) past thresholds, and US 1099-K where applicable. Vinted/eBay/Depop all built seller-info collection flows (TIN, address) triggered by sales volume. ThryftVerse: nothing user-visible. Resolution: threshold-triggered “complete your seller details” flow + annual earnings statements (ties into 4.13 statements) (P1, legally driven).
[136|Heading 2] 5.9 Pricing intelligence & market data
[137|First Paragraph] eBay Terapeak, StockX market data, Vinted’s price suggestions. ThryftVerse’s market-ledger + ml-service make this unusually attainable: sold-comps at listing time (5.5), price-drop suggestions for stale listings (“items like this sell 40% faster under £25”), and portfolio-style “your closet value” (P1–P2).

[200|Heading 1] 9. Competitor Deep-Dive Teardowns
[201|First Paragraph] This section decomposes each benchmark app into the design decisions and business rules that make it flagship-grade, and states specifically what ThryftVerse should learn (and what it should deliberately not copy).
[202|Heading 2] 9.1 Instagram — the engagement architecture benchmark
[203|First Paragraph] What makes it flagship: - Content hierarchy is ruthless. One post owns the viewport; chrome is minimal (username, image, action row, caption). Everything else recedes. The feed is a sequence of full-attention moments, not a dashboard. - The action row grammar — like/comment/share/save in a fixed, muscle-memory order with instant optimistic feedback and a double-tap shortcut. Users perform these actions thousands of times; zero-latency response is non-negotiable. - Stories as a parallel, low-pressure content lane — ephemerality lowers the posting bar; the ring metaphor communicates unseen content in one glance; reply-to-story converts passive viewing into DMs (their strongest retention bridge). - Notification aggregation — “X, Y and 12 others liked your photo” turns noise into flattery. Activity is grouped by type and recency. - Creation is camera-first — the composer opens into capture, not a form.
[204|Body Text] ThryftVerse take-aways: double-tap like exists (DoubleTapHeart) — good. Adopt: story rings with unwatched-first ordering (4.2), reply-to-story → DM (4.10), aggregated notifications (4.14), camera-first sell composer (7.4), product hotspot tags on Looks — Instagram’s shopping tags are the proven pattern for content→commerce (4.12).
[205|Body Text] Do not copy: the algorithmic opacity that frustrates sellers; ThryftVerse should tell sellers why a listing is underperforming (analytics, quality meter) — a trust advantage Instagram never offers.
[206|Heading 2] 9.2 Pinterest — the visual discovery benchmark
[207|First Paragraph] What makes it flagship: - The masonry grid respects image aspect ratios — no crops to uniform tiles; the rhythm of varied heights is what makes browsing feel endless and organic. - Every image is a query. Long-press → visually similar; tap → closeup with “more like this” flowing infinitely below. The user never hits a dead end — every leaf node re-roots the browse tree. - Boards as identity — saving is organising; users curate taste publicly, and boards themselves become follow-able discovery objects. - Interest onboarding — the first-run topic picker immediately personalises the home feed and shows the feed reshaping, teaching the user that engagement improves relevance. - The save micro-animation — the pin flying into a board is a signature delight moment that reinforces the core action.
[208|Body Text] ThryftVerse take-aways: PinterestMasonryGrid exists — make it the default Explore surface with infinite similar-item chaining (3.2, 4.3). Elevate collections toward social boards (4.11). Add the fly-to-board save animation (7.2). Ship interest/size onboarding that visibly reshapes the feed (4.1).
[209|Heading 2] 9.3 TikTok / Whatnot — the live & video commerce benchmark
[210|Compact] TikTok Shop proved video product discovery converts at rates static images cannot; Whatnot proved live auctions are an entertainment format — the auctioneer energy, chat, scarcity timers, and “sudden death” bidding create appointment viewing.
[211|Compact] Whatnot’s core loop: browse scheduled shows → follow sellers → notification when live → join stream → bid in overlay dock → instant checkout with saved payment.
[212|Compact] ThryftVerse’s advantage: it already has the auction engine, sticky bid dock, countdowns, seller auction centre, and group chat infrastructure. The delta to a v1 live show is: RTMP/WebRTC ingest, a live viewer screen with chat overlay (chat system exists), and a “current lot” binding to the existing bid dock. This is the highest-differentiation P2 bet (8).
[213|Heading 2] 9.4 Vinted — the C2C friction-removal benchmark
[214|First Paragraph] Business model: free selling (0% seller fee) funded by a buyer-protection fee (~5% + fixed) — a structural growth weapon that flooded supply. Monetises sellers optionally: bumps, wardrobe spotlight.
[215|Body Text] Flagship mechanics ThryftVerse lacks (all detailed in §5): 1. Integrated shipping: buyer picks carrier/drop-off at checkout → prepaid label/QR to seller → tracking auto-ingested → escrow auto-release 2 days post-delivery. 2. Saved searches with push alerts; size-profile default filters (“only my sizes”). 3. Bundles with seller discount tiers and combined shipping. 4. Escrow + “Everything OK?” confirmation loop that doubles as review capture. 5. Off-platform payment interception in chat (fraud protection). 6. DAC7 seller-info collection flows.
[216|Body Text] Do not copy: Vinted’s utilitarian, low-editorial visual style. ThryftVerse’s editorial/premium art direction is a real differentiator against Vinted’s classifieds aesthetic — keep it.
[217|Heading 2] 9.5 Depop — the social-selling benchmark
[218|Compact] The shop is a brand: sellers merchandise their closets (grid aesthetics matter, pinned items, bio links); top sellers behave like micro-brands. ThryftVerse’s closet boards are close — add shop merchandising controls (4.11).
[219|Compact] The following feed is the home surface: new listings from followed sellers, chronological. This is the single most transplantable retention surface given ThryftVerse’s now-working social graph.
[220|Compact] Boosted listings (8% success fee, no upfront cost) — a low-risk monetisation format sellers accept because it’s pay-on-results; consider over Vinted-style prepaid bumps.
[221|Compact] Share-to-Instagram tooling — Depop grew on sellers advertising their own listings on IG; auto-generated share cards with deep links are cheap growth (6.2, G7).
[222|Heading 2] 9.6 Poshmark — the engagement-mechanics benchmark
[223|Compact] Offer-to-Likers with shipping discount: the platform’s signature conversion machine — every like becomes a lead a seller can activate. Requires: per-listing likers list (exists), offer composer with discount presets, expiring private offers with push.
[224|Compact] Posh Parties (timed themed listing events) create appointment-based community selling — an interesting future fit for ThryftVerse’s auction/drop culture.
[225|Compact] Ambassador programme — status tiers for community behaviour, feeding the seller-standards concept (5.7).
[226|Heading 2] 9.7 eBay — the trust & scale benchmark
[227|Compact] Money Back Guarantee — the strongest trust promise in commerce, backed by the structured Resolution Centre (case types, evidence, deadlines, enforced outcomes) — the model for §5.6.
[228|Compact] Best Offer with auto-accept/decline floors — negotiation that resolves instantly at scale (4.6).
[229|Compact] Proxy bidding — max-bid automation that keeps auctions fair and reduces snipe-anxiety (4.8).
[230|Compact] Promoted Listings — self-serve ad rates with transparent performance reporting; the mature endpoint of the boost roadmap.
[231|Compact] Seller levels & Terapeak pricing analytics — performance standards tied to visibility, and market data as a seller subscription value driver (5.7, 5.9).
[232|Heading 2] 9.8 StockX / Grailed — the authentication & market-data benchmark
[233|Compact] StockX turned verification into the brand: every item flows through authentication; the product page is a market instrument (bid/ask, last-sold, price history charts). ThryftVerse’s co-own vertical is already adjacent — extend market-data UI (sparklines, last-trade) to co-own assets first, then price-history to high-value standard listings (4.9, 4.4).
[234|Compact] Grailed’s dark, editorial, menswear-collector aesthetic demonstrates the premium-resale visual direction; relevant precedent for the dark theme (7.1).
