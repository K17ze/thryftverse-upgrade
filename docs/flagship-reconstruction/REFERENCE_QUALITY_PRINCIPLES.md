# Reference Quality Principles

## Extraction Methodology

Each reference image was analysed for structural and gestalt qualities rather than literal layout copying. The goal is to understand what makes the reference feel high-quality, then adapt those principles to Thryftverse's identity.

## Cross-Reference Principles

### What makes references feel high quality

1. **Restraint in colour** — Limited palette; no competing accents; status colours used sparingly.
2. **Generous whitespace** — Sections breathe; cards have internal padding; text does not touch edges.
3. **Consistent radius language** — Cards, buttons, inputs, avatars follow a strict radius system.
4. **Subtle elevation** — Shadows are soft and directional; never harsh.
5. **Media-first hierarchy** — Images and avatars are larger than text; media frames are intentional.
6. **Clear primary action** — One dominant action per screen; secondary actions are visually subordinate.
7. **Typography as hierarchy** — Size, weight, and colour create reading order without borders.
8. **Status communication** — Unread, active, selected, and error states are immediately legible.
9. **Destructive separation** — Dangerous actions are visually and spatially separated from normal actions.
10. **Empty state guidance** — Empty screens explain what will appear and how to get there.

### How spacing is distributed

- **Screen edge to content**: 16–20px horizontal; never 0.
- **Section to section**: 24–32px vertical; never cramped.
- **Card internal padding**: 16px uniform.
- **Row height**: 56px minimum for tappable rows.
- **Media to content**: 12–16px gap.
- **Form field spacing**: 16px between fields; 8px between label and input.

### How media is prioritised

- Avatars: 56–96px depending on context.
- Cover images: 3:1 or 16:9 aspect; full-bleed or large rounded frame.
- Product images: Square or 4:5; edge-to-edge in cards.
- Thumbnails: 44px minimum for tappable targets.

### How actions are ranked

1. Primary action: full-width or prominent button; highest contrast.
2. Secondary action: outline or text button; medium contrast.
3. Tertiary action: icon-only or inline text; lowest contrast.
4. Destructive action: separated spatially; red colour; always requires confirmation.

### How navigation is communicated

- Back button: 44x44 tappable area; surface background; arrow icon.
- Title: centred or left-aligned; never ambiguous.
- Right action: text or icon; 44x44 minimum.
- Tab bar: 5 items; active state indicated by icon fill + dot.

## Thryftverse-Specific Adaptations

- **No gold/yellow** — Brand uses warm off-white (dark mode) and dark neutral (light mode).
- **No glass-heavy styling** — Surfaces are solid; no blur backgrounds.
- **No imitation of other brands** — Principles are adapted, not copied.
- **Luxury e-commerce restraint** — Less is more; let content breathe.
- **Community-first identity** — Social features (profiles, edits, looks) need editorial richness.
